Ethical

Those who have adopted a vegan lifestyle because they are adamantly opposed to animal cruelty are known as ethical vegans. Such vegans abhor any form of animal exploitation in their daily lives. A third of those who decide to become vegans do so out of compassion for animals. Animals are intelligent beings with a right to life. According to ethical vegans, there is no distinction between a dog and a pig or a cat and a fish. They are all living things with the need to survive and are conscious.

Environmental

These people adopt a vegan diet as a means to live more sustainably and to help the environment. Environmental vegans think that by choosing a vegan diet, they  lessen their impact on deforestation, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and minimize pollution by refusing to support animal agriculture. Are you aware that greenhouse gas emissions from animal husbandry exceed those from all global transportation systems combined?

Health

A vegan diet may reduce the risk of diabetes and cardiovascular disease, according to a research by the medical testing business Medichecks. Participants in the study who had switched to veganism had blood glucose and harmful cholesterol levels that were below average. For health reasons, those with lactose sensitivity may also decide to follow a vegan diet.

Religious

Religious vegans choose their dietary choice based on their spiritual convictions. For instance, Jainism is a religion whose adherents follow a strict vegan diet. It is a tradition from ancient India. All forms of violence are abhorred by Jainists. Their main conviction is that all religious action is meaningless if they do not give up all violent behavior. “One’s ultimate religious responsibility is non-violence,” as a result, which extends to their eating habits. Though not all Buddhists are vegan, there are a few of them. Buddhism has many diverse schools, some of which include vegan practitioners. Some Buddhist sects advise vegetarianism and restrict monks from consuming any sort of meat. In a same vein, 33% of Hindus practice vegetarianism. There are a few Jewish vegans and during lent, some Christians also adopt a vegan or vegetarian diet.
